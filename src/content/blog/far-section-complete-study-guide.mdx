FAR (Financial Accounting & Reporting) is often considered the most challenging CPA exam section. With historically reported difficulty levels among the highest of core sections, it demands respect - and a solid game plan.

## FAR at a Glance

| Metric | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Testing | Continuous (year-round) |
| Duration | 4 hours |
| Questions | 66 MCQs + 8 TBS |
| Difficulty | High |
| Study Hours | 120-150 recommended |

## Content Breakdown

FAR covers the broadest range of accounting topics of any section. Here's what you'll encounter:

### Financial Reporting (25-35%)

This is the meat of FAR and includes:

- **Financial statement preparation** - Balance sheet, income statement, cash flows
- **Revenue recognition** - ASC 606 and its five-step model
- **Lease accounting** - ASC 842 (lessee and lessor)
- **Stock compensation** - Options, restricted stock, performance shares
- **EPS calculations** - Basic and diluted earnings per share

**Pro tip:** Revenue recognition and leases receive significant exam coverage. Develop strong familiarity with the five-step model and know both operating and finance lease calculations well.

### Assets, Liabilities, and Equity (30-40%)

The largest content area covers:

- **Cash and receivables** - Bank reconciliations, allowance for doubtful accounts
- **Inventory** - FIFO, LIFO, weighted average, lower of cost or market
- **Fixed assets** - Depreciation methods, impairment, disposals
- **Intangibles** - Goodwill, R&D, software development costs
- **Liabilities** - Bonds, troubled debt restructuring, contingencies
- **Equity** - Treasury stock, dividends, retained earnings

**Pro tip:** Inventory and fixed asset questions are areas many candidates find approachable once understood. Know your depreciation methods and inventory flow assumptions well.

### Governmental and Nonprofit (20-30%)

Many candidates struggle here because it's different from commercial accounting:

- **Fund accounting** - Governmental, proprietary, fiduciary funds
- **Modified accrual basis** - When to recognize revenue and expenditures
- **Budgetary accounting** - Encumbrances, budget-to-actual
- **GASB standards** - Statement of net position, statement of activities
- **Nonprofit accounting** - Net asset classifications, contribution revenue

**Pro tip:** Don't skip governmental. It's 20-30% of your score. Create a separate study block just for this content.

### Consolidated Financial Statements (15-25%)

The most conceptually challenging area:

- **Acquisition method** - Fair value calculations, goodwill
- **Intercompany eliminations** - Sales, profits, receivables
- **NCI (noncontrolling interest)** - Calculation and presentation
- **Equity method investments** - Significant influence situations
- **Variable interest entities** - Consolidation requirements

**Pro tip:** Consolidation simulations are commonly encountered. Practice the full consolidation worksheet process, not just individual eliminations.

## Effective Study Strategy

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

Focus on understanding concepts before memorization:

1. **Watch/read core lectures** - Get the big picture first
2. **Take notes in your own words** - Don't just copy
3. **Do MCQs after each topic** - Immediate application cements learning
4. **Don't move on until you understand** - Confusion compounds

### Phase 2: Deep Dive (Weeks 5-8)

Now drill into the details:

1. **Increase MCQ volume** - Aim for 50-100 per day
2. **Start TBS practice** - Simulations require different skills
3. **Focus on weak areas** - Your review software should identify these
4. **Create summary sheets** - Condense key formulas and rules

### Phase 3: Review (Weeks 9-10)

Final preparation phase:

1. **Take full practice exams** - Simulate real conditions
2. **Review wrong answers deeply** - Understand why you missed them
3. **Light review of all topics** - Don't let anything go stale
4. **Rest the final 2-3 days** - Mental freshness matters

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Skimping on Governmental

"I'll just learn enough to get by" often leads to difficulties. Governmental content is:
- Frequently emphasized
- Conceptually different from commercial accounting
- More approachable once you understand fund types

### Mistake 2: Passive Studying

Watching lectures isn't studying. You must:
- Do practice questions actively
- Work through problems without looking at answers first
- Teach concepts to yourself or others

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Simulations

TBS are worth 50% of your score. Many candidates:
- Only do MCQs until the last week
- Don't practice using the actual exam interface
- Can't manage time on complex simulations

### Mistake 4: Not Memorizing Key Formulas

Some things just need to be memorized:
- Diluted EPS calculation
- Lease calculations (PV factors provided, but process isn't)
- Pension expense components
- Bank reconciliation format

## Must-Know Formulas

### Diluted EPS
```
Diluted EPS = (Net Income + Convertible Adjustments) /
              (Weighted Shares + Dilutive Securities)
```

### Bond Premium/Discount Amortization
```
Interest Expense = Carrying Value × Market Rate
Cash Paid = Face Value × Stated Rate
Amortization = Difference
```

### Depreciation (Double-Declining)
```
Depreciation = (2 / Useful Life) × Beginning Book Value
```
